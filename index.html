The manipulation of fire

Humans discovered fire around 1.7-2 million years ago. The impact of fire is arguably humanities greatest discovery as it not only affected our evolution but our advancement in technology. One theory rooted with our manipulation of fire is that when we cooked our food, we could spend less energy digesting and absorb more nutrients. As a result our brain grew in size allowing increase in group sizes and social cognition.

Communication

What separates us humans from apes is that we have a voicebox, lower in the throat, rounder tongue and soft palate that allow us to make more sounds than apes. Cerebral Cortex

What separates the human brain from the brain of other species? Humans are undoubtedly the smartest species in terms of cognitive ability and processing power. Our complex unique minds can be largely attributed to our cerebral cortex which controls human senses, thoughts, language and memory. In terms of our cognitive process. Due to our large cerebral cortex, human thought processes separate us from the rest of the animal kingdom.
